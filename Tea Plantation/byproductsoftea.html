Tea Tree Oil

Australia's Queensland and New South Wales are home to the little Melaleuca alternifolia tree. Tea tree oil is made from its leaves. Melaleuca alternifolia, sometimes known as the tea tree, should not be confused with the plant whose leaves are used to make black, green, and oolong tea. Tea tree oil has long been used as a traditional remedy by the Aboriginal population of Australia. These indigenous Australians smash the tea tree leaves to obtain the oil, which they then either apply directly to wounds to promote healing or inhale to combat colds and coughs. Today, simple oil, the most basic type of tea tree oil, is commonly available. For skin care products, there are also diluted variations available with potencies ranging from 5 to 50%. Many components of tea tree oil, most notably terpinen-4-ol, have been shown to be effective against particular bacteria, viruses, and fungi. When terpinen-4-ol is present, your white blood cells, which help protect against diseases and other outside invaders, seem to work more vigorously. Tea tree oil is generally regarded as a natural remedy for bacterial and fungal skin disorders because of its capacity to eradicate germs. Additionally, it helps to speed up recovery and prevent infection.

Uses of Tea Tree Oil

  2. Clean and Kill germs
  3. Get rid of Fungus
  4. Mold be gone
